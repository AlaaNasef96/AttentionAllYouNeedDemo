The one-minute version

A model that learns what to look at,
not what came before.

Before 2017, translation models read a sentence one word at a time, like a tape passing under a head. The Transformer threw that away. Every word looks at every other word, all at once, and decides what matters.

01 / parallel

No recurrence

Drop the step-by-step loop. The whole sentence is processed simultaneously, so training scales across GPUs instead of crawling word by word.

02 / attention

Everything connects

Self-attention lets any word draw information directly from any other — distance is no longer a barrier to meaning.

03 / state of the art

Faster and better

Trained in a fraction of the time, it still beat the best recurrent and convolutional systems on machine translation.

Why the old way struggled

The problem with reading in order

Recurrent networks pass a hidden "memory" forward, one token at a time. Information from the first word has to survive a long relay to reach the last. The signal fades, and nothing can be computed until the previous step finishes.

Recurrent — sequential

One word at a time

  • Step t must wait for step t−1 — no parallelism
  • Long-range links cross many steps — signal decays
  • Path between two words grows with distance
Self-attention — all at once

Every word, together

  • All positions computed in parallel — GPU-friendly
  • Any word reaches any other in a single hop
  • Constant path length, regardless of distance

Order without a clock

How does it know word order?

If everything is processed at once, how does the model tell "dog bites man" from "man bites dog"? It adds a positional encoding — a unique fingerprint of sine and cosine waves stamped onto each position. Each row below is one position; each column, one dimension.

← embedding dimension (0 → 64) →position ↓
PE(pos, 2i) = sin(pos / 100002i/d)
PE(pos,2i+1) = cos(pos / 100002i/d)

Low dimensions wave slowly, high dimensions quickly — like the hour, minute, and second hands of a clock. Together they give every position a signature the model can read, and even extrapolate to lengths it never saw in training.

Why it still matters
Every model with a "GPT", a "BERT", or a "T" in its name is a descendant of this single architecture.

The Transformer wasn't just a better translator. By making attention the whole engine, it scaled in ways recurrence never could — and became the foundation of modern large language models, image generators, protein folders, and code assistants. This page was rendered by one of its descendants.
